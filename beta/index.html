Discover Europe's last wilderness

A Polar Expedition it doesn't have to be to the North or South pole. There are places much closer to home and less extreme. Northern Sweden is an amazing place that offers beautiful landscapes and is more accessible. It's a place where you can look and not see anything or anyone around you - that's why they call it the Europe's last wilderness.

Kungsleden

Our Swedish expeditions take place on the northern section of the Kungsleden (Kings Trail). The whole trail continues for 440km, but we use the best part of it and it's around 90km.

The landscape is very beautiful but we must not forget that the weather can change in a matter of minutes. It can completely shift form a sunny day to a total whiteout. If you don't know where you are and how to behave, you can get yourself in trouble. Luckily we come prepared. You're in good hands.

No matter the weather - it's a meditative experience.
Just put one foot in front of the other. Many times you can just let your mind go blank. Even tough you're in an cold environment, you're not cold because your body generates a lot of heat through of the rhythmic movement. It's almost magic.

Why join an expedition?

Everyone has their own reasons for seeking out a trip like this. During the golden age of exploration, seemingly ordinary men achieved extraordinary things. One of the first two men that stood on top of Everest, Sir Edmund Hillary was a beekeeper by trade. What made them different was the drive for exploration.

These days our lives are so effortless. We live in cities with everything we might desire available to us. Because of our comforts we loose touch with nature and with ourselves. Literally getting out of your comfort zone can help you reconnect with yourself and nature, while still having the safety and comfort of a hut or tent at night.

Why do you want to join? Is it the physical and mental challenge? Is it for the scenery, or just some piece and quiet?
